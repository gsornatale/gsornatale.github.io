BUSTY AND THE BASS

The Troubadour - Los Angeles

February 28th, 2018

Busty and the Bass in Los Angeles was a pretty special show. One, because the concert was dope… They killed it! Their energy was amazing, the crowd danced and they were genuinely excited to be performing at the Troubadour for us. But it was important to me because this was the first concert that I ever photographed. On top of that, I’ve been learning how to code (hence this gorgeous site) and this show was finally going to give me something I wanted to kick my digital journal off with and make available to the World Wide Web! All my hard work was finally coming together and I finally started to weasel my way into a creative world I’m really enjoying. Here’s the kicker…. I left my camera on the LA 704 westbound bus THE DAY BEFORE THE SHOW! The show I’ve been looking forward to for weeks, SO I COULD PHOTOGRAPH IT, suddenly went from something that excited the hell out of me, to something that made me furious at the world, but mostly myself.

If you know me, and if I know myself, I leave shit around. So before you even start… shut up. I know, it’s a bad habit and I’m working on it! I had my face buried in a book called ‘Neverwhere’ (thanks for the recommendation, Cameron), the camera was sitting next to me because it wouldn’t fit in my bag and I noticed the doors were about to close at my stop so I jumped off and didn’t realize I left it until I was home. I’m crossing my fingers and putting faith into the universe that a kind soul gave the driver a very expensive, meaningful piece of equipment they knew was not theirs to keep. Again, finger’s crossed. So now what?

I rented a camera. I couldn’t let the day be ruined by my bullshit. A friend pointed out this website called ShareGrid where people rent out their personal gear for a daily rate. I got pretty much the same camera I once had (RIP) and a dope lens (for way more than I wanted to pay) from these two awesome guys at Rare Breeds in Santa Monica. They were a huge help in walking me through some things I didn't know (which, right now, is pretty much everything). Also, shout out to my friend, Sam, who gave me an amazing 101 to concert photography. I’m excited to get my camera back one day and get into a lot more concerts. Photos from the night below.

Oh, and thanks to Gina, for being my beautiful model while I played around with the new equipment.

The pictures below were shot with a Sony A6500 with a Sony G-Master f-2.8 70-200 Lens.
